# We Brand Schools

## Phase 1: Requirements and Policy Freeze

### Purpose

`We Brand Schools` is a public-facing initiative by Dexta to help schools establish a professional online presence by offering a free school website built from approved templates.

This document freezes the phase 1 business rules, product scope, and operational assumptions so design, development, content, and admin workflow can move forward without ambiguity.

## 1. Product Goal

The goal of the initiative is to:

- help schools get online faster with a professional website,
- reduce the cost barrier for schools that need a digital presence,
- provide Dexta with a structured, repeatable system for onboarding schools through templates rather than custom website builds.

## 2. Core Offer

### What "Free Website" Includes

Each approved school receives:

- one website built from an existing approved `We Brand Schools` template,
- template customization using the school's name, logo, colors, text, images, and official details,
- up to 6 core pages unless a chosen template naturally uses fewer:
  - Home
  - About
  - Admissions
  - Academics or Programs
  - News or Events
  - Contact
- mobile-responsive layout,
- basic on-page SEO setup:
  - page titles
  - meta descriptions
  - favicon
  - social sharing image where available
- integration of the school's official phone numbers, email addresses, address, and social links,
- one contact or inquiry form,
- deployment of the website after all required content is approved,
- basic admin-side tracking of job progress from application to live status.

### What "Free Website" Does Not Include

The free offer does not include:

- a custom design from scratch,
- unlimited revisions,
- a full school management portal,
- student result checker,
- e-learning platform,
- payment gateway integration,
- bespoke animations or advanced interactive features,
- copywriting from scratch for the school,
- photography, videography, or logo design,
- paid stock assets,
- multilingual setup unless already supported by the template,
- post-launch continuous content management,
- domain purchase fees,
- hosting fees beyond the initial agreed support window,
- third-party software subscription fees.

## 3. Eligibility and Approval Policy

To keep the initiative sustainable, submissions are applications, not automatic approvals.

### Eligible Applicants

The initiative is intended for:

- nursery schools,
- primary schools,
- secondary schools,
- colleges,
- academies,
- training institutes,
- faith-based schools,
- private and public schools that can provide verifiable official contact details.

### Approval Conditions

Dexta may approve or decline an application based on:

- completeness of submitted information,
- professionalism and legitimacy of the school,
- availability of internal capacity,
- suitability of the selected template for the school's needs,
- availability of a workable domain option,
- compliance with Dexta's brand and operational standards.

### Decline Conditions

An application may be declined if:

- the school submits false or unverifiable information,
- the project requires a custom build beyond the free offer,
- the applicant refuses to provide required content,
- the requested domain is problematic or misleading,
- the school expects features outside the free package,
- Dexta is at capacity.

## 4. Template Policy

### Template Use

- Schools must choose from available templates displayed on the landing page.
- A school can preview screenshots and videos before selecting a template.
- Template structure remains fixed.
- Content, branding assets, colors, and media can be adapted within the template's supported areas.

### Customization Limits

Allowed customization:

- school logo,
- school name,
- school colors where supported,
- school images and videos,
- school text content,
- school contact details,
- school social links.

Not allowed under the free plan:

- changing the entire layout architecture,
- mixing multiple templates into one build,
- requesting a brand-new template,
- adding new complex modules outside the selected template,
- major structural page changes after implementation has started.

## 5. Domain Policy

The application flow must support two domain paths.

### Option A: School Already Has a Domain

If the school already has a domain:

- the school must provide the domain name,
- the school is responsible for proving access or ownership,
- Dexta will use the provided domain only after verification,
- DNS connection support may be provided during deployment if needed.

### Option B: School Does Not Have a Domain

If the school does not have a domain:

- the school must submit a preferred domain name,
- the school must also submit a second preferred domain name,
- domain availability is not guaranteed,
- final domain choice depends on availability and approval,
- Dexta may recommend alternatives close to the submitted preference.

### Domain Cost Policy

To avoid ambiguity, the recommended freeze for now is:

- domain registration is not included in the free website offer,
- the school is responsible for paying for domain registration,
- Dexta can guide the school on the domain process,
- if Dexta decides to sponsor some domains later, that should be treated as a separate campaign decision, not a default policy.

## 6. Hosting Policy

Recommended operational rule:

- initial deployment/setup is included,
- long-term hosting cost is not included by default,
- schools must either:
  - provide an existing hosting arrangement, or
  - pay for hosting after approval.

If Dexta chooses to host some projects temporarily, that should be clearly described as a limited support window rather than permanent free hosting.

### Frozen Hosting Language

For build and product consistency, use this rule:

- website design, setup, and launch are included,
- domain and long-term hosting are separate responsibilities unless explicitly stated otherwise for a specific approved school.

## 7. Content Submission Policy

The school is responsible for providing the content needed to complete the site.

### Required Submission Fields

The application form must collect:

- school name,
- about the school,
- vision,
- mission,
- core values,
- official contact details,
- selected template,
- domain choice path,
- current domain or preferred domains depending on selection.

### Official Contact Details Must Include

- official phone number,
- official email address,
- school address,
- optional website/social links if already available,
- main contact person name,
- main contact person role,
- main contact person phone number,
- main contact person email.

### Content Readiness Rule

- Work should not move into build execution until the required content is complete.
- Incomplete content can remain in `PENDING`.
- Admin can move a job to `IN_PROGRESS` only when content is sufficient to begin implementation.

## 8. Revision Policy

To protect capacity, revisions must be limited.

### Recommended Freeze

The free package includes:

- 1 initial implementation based on submitted content,
- up to 2 minor revision rounds before go-live.

Minor revisions include:

- text corrections,
- image swaps,
- contact detail updates,
- small content rearrangements within the template.

Minor revisions do not include:

- choosing a different template after build has started,
- redesigning page sections,
- adding entirely new pages,
- adding new features outside template scope.

Any request beyond the free revision allowance should be treated as a paid upgrade or a separate project.

## 9. Turnaround Time Policy

Turnaround depends on approval and content completeness.

### Recommended Freeze

- review of a new application: within 2 to 5 business days,
- website production after approval and complete content: 7 to 14 business days,
- revision review turnaround: 2 to 4 business days per round.

### Delay Rule

If a school delays content, approval, or feedback:

- the turnaround clock pauses until the required response is received.

## 10. Post-Launch Support Policy

The free website offer should include limited post-launch support only.

### Recommended Freeze

Included after launch:

- bug fixes related to the delivered template setup,
- contact detail corrections,
- launch support for a limited 14-day window.

Not included after launch:

- ongoing site management,
- regular content updates,
- redesign requests,
- feature expansion,
- SEO campaign management,
- long-term maintenance without a separate agreement.

## 11. Public User Flow Requirements

The public experience must follow this flow:

1. User lands on `We Brand Schools` page.
2. User reads the offer, benefits, and application steps.
3. User browses available website templates.
4. User opens a template to view screenshots and videos.
5. User sees a persistent `Select Template` button while previewing.
6. User selects a template.
7. A modal opens with form step 1:
   - about your school
   - vision
   - mission
   - core values
   - official contact detail
8. User clicks `Next`.
9. Form step 2 slides in and asks whether the school already has a domain.
10. If `Yes`, user provides the existing domain name.
11. If `No`, user provides:

- preferred domain name
- second preferred domain name

12. User submits the application.
13. User sees a success state confirming the request was received.

## 12. Admin Workflow Requirements

Admin must be able to manage the initiative without touching code.

### Admin Content Management

Admin must be able to:

- edit landing page content,
- change section titles and descriptions,
- update CTA text,
- change the landing page logo,
- update help/contact information shown on the page.

### Admin Template Management

Admin must be able to:

- add a new template,
- edit a template,
- delete a template,
- toggle visibility of a template,
- upload multiple images for a template,
- upload multiple videos for a template,
- define the live preview URL,
- add template description and feature summary,
- order templates for display.

### Admin Application Management

Admin must be able to:

- see all submitted applications in a table,
- click into a record to view full submitted details,
- change job/application status,
- optionally add internal notes,
- identify selected template and domain path quickly.

### Frozen Status Set

The application/job statuses are:

- `PENDING` with yellow badge
- `IN_PROGRESS` with blue badge
- `LIVE` with green badge
- `DECLINED` with red badge

### Status Meaning

- `PENDING`: application received but not yet approved for execution
- `IN_PROGRESS`: approved and currently being worked on
- `LIVE`: website completed and deployed
- `DECLINED`: not approved or not moving forward

## 13. Communication Policy

The system should make it clear that:

- submission does not guarantee approval,
- Dexta will review each application,
- schools may be contacted for clarification,
- launch timing depends on content readiness and approval.

Recommended public language:

`Apply to get your school website built from one of our ready-to-launch templates. Every submission is reviewed before production begins.`

## 14. Non-Functional Requirements

The feature should be built with these quality standards:

- mobile-first and fully responsive,
- accessible form and modal interactions,
- clear loading, success, and error states,
- validation for required fields,
- secure server-side submission handling,
- media assets optimized for performance,
- admin interface designed for non-technical staff.

## 15. Frozen Decisions for Phase 2 Build

Unless changed by explicit stakeholder approval, development should proceed with these assumptions:

- the free offer uses approved templates only,
- domain registration is not free by default,
- long-term hosting is not free by default,
- schools provide their own content,
- schools get up to 2 minor revision rounds,
- launch support is limited to 14 days after go-live,
- every submission is reviewed before approval,
- admin manages templates, applications, page content, and logo from the dashboard,
- one application is tied to one selected template,
- application status uses only `PENDING`, `IN_PROGRESS`, `LIVE`, and `DECLINED`.

## 16. Items to Confirm Before Coding Starts

These are not blockers, but they should be confirmed by you before phase 2 is finalized:

- whether schools should upload their logo and media during the initial form or after approval,
- whether admin notes should be visible only internally,
- whether automatic email acknowledgements should be sent on submission,
- whether a school can submit more than one application,
- whether the live website URL should be shown publicly on the template card after launch.
